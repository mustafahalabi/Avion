# Purpose

This document defines every department within Engineering OS.

A department is an organizational unit responsible for a permanent business capability.

Departments own outcomes.

Employees execute work on behalf of departments.

Departments provide organizational stability while employees, processes, and tools evolve.

---

# Why This Document Exists

Engineering organizations scale through specialization.

Instead of assigning work directly to individuals, Engineering OS assigns responsibility to departments.

Departments provide:

* Clear ownership
* Consistent standards
* Shared knowledge
* Stable collaboration
* Long-term accountability

Every employee belongs to exactly one primary department.

# Department Principles

Every department exists because it owns expertise that permanently benefits the organization.

Departments should:

* Own outcomes
* Define standards
* Share knowledge
* Collaborate openly
* Continuously improve

Departments should never compete with one another.

The company succeeds together.

# Cross-Department Collaboration

Departments collaborate continuously.

Typical collaboration paths include:

Product ↔ Engineering

Engineering ↔ Quality

Quality ↔ Operations

Operations ↔ Executive

Growth ↔ Product

Engineering ↔ Growth

Communication should occur directly between departments.

The CEO should not coordinate day-to-day collaboration.

---

# Department Ownership Rules

Every initiative has one owning department.

Other departments contribute when necessary.

Ownership remains constant unless formally transferred.

Departments cannot permanently delegate accountability.

---

# Department Standards

Every department maintains:

* Internal standards
* Department knowledge
* Department metrics
* Department retrospectives
* Improvement initiatives

Departments are responsible for improving themselves over time.

---

# Creating New Departments

A new department may only be created when:

* A permanent organizational capability emerges.
* Existing departments cannot reasonably absorb the responsibility.
* Long-term ownership would otherwise become unclear.

Temporary initiatives should not create permanent departments.

---

# Department Health

Every department is continuously evaluated using:

* Quality of outcomes
* Collaboration effectiveness
* Knowledge maturity
* Documentation quality
* Decision quality
* Delivery consistency
* Continuous improvement

Poor departmental health should trigger organizational improvements rather than individual blame.

---

# Definition of a Healthy Department

A healthy department:

* Has a clearly defined mission.
* Owns measurable outcomes.
* Collaborates effectively.
* Maintains documentation.
* Continuously learns.
* Produces predictable results.
* Improves organizational knowledge.

Departments exist to create clarity.

Not bureaucracy.

---

# Final Principle

Employees are temporary participants in the organization.

Departments are permanent organizational capabilities.

Engineering OS grows by strengthening departments, not by increasing individual heroics.
